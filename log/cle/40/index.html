Conclusions

Average error -1.3 degrees.
In the evening session, unfortunately my table got moved - my father-in-law had to mow the lawn! I took more time with the GPS to try and align the table than previously.
I couldn't read YTR clearly as CLB 216 was very strong on that channel. I thought about taking a set of readings on 216-CLB however, since I thought that YTR may still have an effect on the reading and make it read in error. And frankly, it's been a long day - and I'm ready for bed!
Also noted, there was a strong carrier near the 263-YGK signal which was pulling down the AGC for the set as I moved it - my initial reading was so far out I cheated and re-measured. I suspect this carrier / AGC issue was responsible for the +35 degree error during the daytime session too, but once wise to its effect I listened more closely for quality of signal rather than just volume.
Overall I noticed no real difference between the two sessions, other than perhaps concluding my 'True North' marker may have been better aligned the second time.
